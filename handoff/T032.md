## Summary

Implemented the compliance-service as a zero-dependency Go module at `src/compliance-service/`. The service implements participant onboarding workflows (APPLICATION_SUBMITTED → DOCUMENTS_PENDING → DOCUMENTS_UPLOADED → VERIFICATION_IN_PROGRESS → SCREENING_IN_PROGRESS → RISK_SCORING → APPROVED/MANUAL_REVIEW/REJECTED), document verification, watchlist screening with a pluggable provider interface, risk scoring engine with 6 weighted factors and 4 tiers (Low/Medium/High/Prohibited), monitoring alerts, and SAR filing. Ports: gRPC 50056, health HTTP 8086. All 43 tests pass with 80%+ coverage on business logic.

## Key Decisions

1. **Pluggable screening provider interface** — `screening.Provider` interface allows swapping between `DefaultProvider` (dev/no-op), `StaticProvider` (testing), and future production providers (Dow Jones, Refinitiv) without changing core logic. Follows T015 spec's adapter pattern.
2. **Risk-based auto-approve/escalate** — Low/Medium risk participants are auto-approved; High risk goes to MANUAL_REVIEW; Prohibited auto-rejects. Matches the T015 spec's tiered approach.
3. **In-memory stores with interface abstraction** — Both `onboarding.Store` and `screening.Store` interfaces allow swapping to PostgreSQL implementations later. The in-memory versions are fully functional for development and testing.
4. **Separate onboarding and screening packages** — Clean separation of concerns: onboarding handles the application lifecycle and documents; screening handles watchlist checks, risk scoring, alerts, and SARs.
5. **Country risk database** — Hard-coded high-risk (KP, IR, SY, AF, YE) and medium-risk (MM, LY, SO, SD, VE) countries. Production should use a configurable country risk database.
6. **24-hour screening cache** — Recent screening results (< 24h) are reused unless force rescreen is requested, reducing unnecessary API calls to screening providers.
