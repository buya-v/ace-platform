## Summary

Built a SPAN-style margin calculation engine for commodity futures as a standalone Go module (`margin-engine`). The engine evaluates positions against 16 SPAN scanning scenarios (price moves at 1/3, 2/3, and full scan range with volatility shifts, plus 2 extreme moves at reduced weight), calculates mark-to-market P&L, delivery month charges, and generates margin calls when collateral is insufficient. All 40 tests pass including concurrency tests.

## Key Decisions

1. **Separate Go module (`margin-engine`)** — follows the clearing-engine/matching-engine pattern with its own `go.mod`, zero external dependencies, and identical Decimal type for cross-service compatibility.
2. **Standard 16 SPAN scenarios** — implements the CME SPAN methodology: 14 standard scenarios scanning 1/3, 2/3, 3/3 of price scan range × vol up/down, plus 2 extreme scenarios (3× scan range) at 30% weight.
3. **Scanning risk = worst-case loss** — for each position, evaluates P&L across all scenarios and takes the maximum loss as the scanning risk. This is the core SPAN primitive.
4. **Mark-to-market reduces margin** — unrealized profits reduce the margin requirement; unrealized losses increase it. Total required = initial margin - MtM (floored at zero).
5. **Delivery month charge** — instruments in delivery month incur an additional charge (rate × spot price × contract size × quantity), reflecting increased risk near expiry.
6. **Margin call lifecycle** — calls are issued when collateral < required, updated if deficit changes, automatically resolved when collateral becomes sufficient, and breached if deadline passes.
7. **Collateral source as interface** — `CollateralSource` is an interface, allowing the engine to be wired to any collateral/treasury service. Dev uses in-memory stub.
8. **Server on port 50053/8083** — avoids collision with matching-engine (50051/8081) and clearing-engine (50052/8082).
